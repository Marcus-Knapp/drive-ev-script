Script 1 — Event Invite: Warm / Existing Hyundai Customer
Highest-converting this week. For anyone who has owned or enquired about a Hyundai.
Opening — First 15 Seconds
"Hi, is that [Name]? It is [Your Name] calling from Read Hyundai. I will not take up much of your time — I am calling about something time-sensitive. Have you noticed what has happened to petrol and diesel prices this week?"
Wait for their response. Almost every driver will say yes. Let them speak briefly, then pivot with energy.
The Hook — Make It Real
"Exactly. Petrol is sitting around 157p a litre, diesel has pushed to nearly 190p — up 25 pence since February because of what is happening in the Middle East. A 55-litre tank now costs over £100 at the pumps. Analysts are saying it will not come back down soon. That is actually exactly why I am calling today."
The Event Pitch
"We are running a special Hyundai EV Event from Wednesday 14th through to Sunday 18th May — this week only. We have the entire electric range in: the brand new IONIQ 9 seven-seater, the IONIQ 5 and IONIQ 6, the KONA Electric, even the brilliant little INSTER. Test drives on every model, no waiting around.

The reason I specifically wanted to call you is that as a Hyundai customer, you qualify for our Q2 Loyalty Bonus — £2,000 off any electric vehicle. That is not available to the general public. We want to make switching as rewarding as possible for people who already trust Hyundai."
Do not rush the loyalty angle. Let the £2,000 land before moving to the close.
The Ask — Get the Booking
"I would love to get you in for a completely no-pressure test drive. We can sit down and run your actual numbers — exactly what you would save on fuel and tax every year. What does your diary look like between the 14th and the 18th? Would mornings or afternoons work better?"
Always give two time options — morning vs afternoon. Never ask "are you free?" — that invites a no.
If They Hesitate
"Absolutely no pressure. Even if you are not planning to change the car right now, with fuel at these levels it is worth knowing your options. Come in, have a coffee, sit in the new IONIQ 9 — it costs you nothing. What is the worst that can happen?"
Close — Confirm Everything
"Brilliant — I will book you in for [Day, Date, Time]. Best number to reach you on? Email or text for confirmation? Looking forward to seeing you, [Name] — I think you will be genuinely impressed."

Why Drive an EV?
10 powerful reasons — use these on every call when a customer needs convincing.
💰
1. Lower Running Costs
Charging at home costs roughly 3–5p per mile vs 15–20p per mile in petrol. On 10,000 miles a year that is over £1,000 saved in fuel alone.
🔧
2. Reduced Maintenance
No oil changes, no spark plugs, no timing belt, no exhaust. Regenerative braking means brake pads last far longer. Fewer parts = fewer bills.
🌿
3. Zero Tailpipe Emissions
Better air quality near schools and in cities. Many customers feel strongly about this — do not skip it.
🏙
4. No ULEZ / CAZ Charges
EVs exempt from Clean Air Zone charges across UK cities. For London commuters this alone can save thousands per year.
🏢
5. Company Car Tax (BIK)
BIK rate for EVs is just 3% vs 25–37% for petrol/diesel. A higher-rate taxpayer in an IONIQ 5 saves £4,000–£6,000/year vs a comparable petrol car.
⚡
6. Instant Torque
Full torque from 0 rpm — smooth, silent, rapid. The IONIQ 5 AWD does 0–62 in 5.1 sec. Customers who test drive rarely go back.
🔌
7. Wake Up With a Full Tank
Plug in at home overnight. Most owners never visit a public charger for daily use — like a petrol station on your driveway, every morning.
📡
8. Growing Charging Network
UK public chargers grew by 45% in 2023 alone. Zap-Map, Pod Point, and Gridserve make finding chargers easy. Range anxiety is becoming a myth.
🛡
9. Hyundai 8-Year Battery Warranty
8 years / 100,000 miles. Batteries typically retain 70–80% capacity after 8–10 years. Hyundai covers you if they do not.
📅
10. Future-Proof Your Purchase
UK ban on new petrol/diesel sales by 2035. EV resale values strengthening as demand rises. Buy now and stay ahead of the curve.

EV Q&A — Customer Questions Answered
Every common question from your event guide, with scripted answers ready to use on calls.
🚗
Will an electric vehicle work for me?
The most common first question
The Answer
"For the vast majority of UK drivers — yes. Most modern EVs do 150–385 miles on a charge, and the average UK driver covers under 25 miles a day. If you can charge at home or at work, you plug in overnight and wake up with a full tank every morning. The only drivers for whom an EV is not yet ideal are those who regularly drive 300+ miles without stopping, or who have no access to home or workplace charging."
💷
Are electric cars expensive to run?
The number that changes minds
The Answer
"Far cheaper than petrol or diesel. Home charging costs around 3–5p per mile. Petrol at 157p/litre costs 15–20p per mile. That is a 4–5x difference. A full charge on an IONIQ 5 at home costs around £6–£15 and covers 250–354 miles. Compare that to £60–£100 for a tank of petrol. No oil changes, no exhaust — servicing costs less too."
⏳
How long does it take to charge?
The most misunderstood topic
The Key Reframe
"The question most people ask is how long charging takes. The better question is: when do you ever need to charge quickly? Most IONIQ owners charge at home overnight and wake up to a full charge. They never visit a public charger for daily use. It is like a phone — you charge it when you are not using it. And when you do need a rapid top-up on a long journey, the IONIQ 5, 6 and 9 all support 800V charging — 10 to 80% in 18–24 minutes. That is a coffee stop."

Closes the biggest single objection
The Answer
"Hyundai's EV batteries are designed to last 8–15 years. Hyundai backs that with an 8-year / 100,000-mile battery warranty. Batteries lose capacity slowly — most retain 70–80% capacity after 8–10 years. If your IONIQ 5 does 354 miles when new, it might do 280–300 miles after a decade. That is still very usable. Tips: keep the battery between 20–80% for daily use, use home slow charging where possible, and avoid running it completely flat."

Do EVs break down more than petrol cars?
Reliability concern
The Answer
"No — in fact the opposite. EVs have far fewer moving parts: no oil to change, no spark plugs, no timing belt, no exhaust. Regenerative braking means even brake pads last significantly longer. Fewer parts = fewer things to go wrong. The most common reason EV drivers call roadside assistance is running low on charge in early ownership — not a mechanical fault — and that quickly becomes a non-issue."
